Pregnancy-Safe Dining Guide

Eating Well in Key West

Seafood-heavy menus, Florida heat — here’s what to know

Safe
Fully cooked seafood
Shrimp, crab, lobster, scallops, and grilled fish cooked through are all fine. Mahi-mahi, grouper, and snapper are low-mercury choices common on every Key West menu.
Avoid
Raw shellfish & raw fish
Raw oysters, clams, sushi, and ceviche should be skipped. Bacterial and parasitic risk is elevated during pregnancy — ask your server how dishes are prepared.
Avoid
High-mercury fish
Shark, swordfish, king mackerel, and tilefish are off the list entirely. These occasionally appear on Florida menus — confirm with your server if unsure.
Limit
Tuna (albacore / yellowfin)
Max 6 oz per week across the whole trip. Tuna tacos and tuna steaks are popular here — enjoy occasionally, but don’t have it every day.
Limit
Mahi-mahi & snapper
Low mercury but best limited to 2–3 servings per week. Both are everywhere in Key West — great choices, just rotate through the trip.
Water
Hydrate constantly
Florida heat plus pregnancy means hydration is non-negotiable. Aim for 10+ cups daily. Ask for a large water at every restaurant — they’ll refill without prompting.
Mocktail
Virgin drinks are everywhere
Virgin mojitos, fruit spritzers, and non-alcoholic frozen drinks are available at nearly every bar and restaurant in Key West. Just ask — no one bats an eye.
Caffeine
Keep it under 200 mg/day
One regular coffee or two to three teas. Cold brew can run higher than expected — check the size before ordering.
Tip
Ask your server — they’re used to it
Key West restaurants are accustomed to dietary requests. Don’t hesitate to ask how fish is prepared or whether a dish can be modified. They’re happy to help.
